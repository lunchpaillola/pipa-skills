# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Project managers, delivery leads, implementation operators, and AI-assisted reviewers who need to understand a long plan or document without sitting at a desk. They may listen while walking, commuting, or scanning on a phone, then return to the transcript to verify wording.

## Product Purpose

`pm-plan-audio-briefing` turns long PM-adjacent source material into a local, single-page listening handoff. The page succeeds when the audio and transcript are immediately usable, and source context is available without becoming another interface to manage.

## Brand Personality

Focused, practical, calm, and low-brand. The generated page should feel closer to a shared working document than a branded listening app, dashboard, marketing page, or decorative demo. Multiple visual directions are valid, but each direction must protect the same core workflow: listen first, skim transcript second, inspect details only when needed.

## Anti-references

Avoid dashboard clutter, repeated SaaS card grids, hero-metric layouts, decorative gradients, badges, pills, pop-outs, and metadata-heavy pages that bury the audio or transcript. Do not make provenance, key points, follow-up prompts, or copy widgets compete with the listening experience.

## Design Principles

1. Lead with the listening job: audio controls and transcript are the primary interface.
2. Collapse supporting material: source context and caveats should be brief, quiet, and secondary.
3. Prefer reading rhythm over section count: fewer regions, better hierarchy, less chrome.
4. Keep generated output self-contained and local-first.
5. Make room for visual exploration without changing the durable page contract.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Use sensible accessibility defaults: readable type, strong contrast, keyboard-friendly native controls, clear focus states, large tap targets on mobile, and reduced-motion-safe behavior.
