# Demand Discovery

Demand discovery finds recent public questions that the canonical post may help answer. V1 is discovery-only.

## When To Use

Use demand discovery for practical/how-to, technical/build, and operator posts with clear answerable topics.

Do not automatically run discovery for conceptual essays, product announcements, or community-specific posts unless the user asks and the query can remain public, relevant, and non-promotional.

## Reddit V1 Rules

- Reddit is read-only discovery in V1.
- Do not post, comment, submit, vote, DM, create remote drafts, or interact with Reddit.
- Search only a small, recent, relevant result set.
- Include community rules or norms when available.
- Avoid ranking by promotional opportunity alone; prefer relevance, recency, and usefulness.
- Generate bespoke answer outlines or local drafts for human review, not copy-paste mass comments.
- Warn the user to adapt any response to community norms before posting manually.

## Query Privacy

Public canonical source material can be used for public read-only discovery before publish approval.

For pasted Markdown, local files, private/paywalled URLs, or unpublished drafts, ask before sending derived titles, excerpts, summaries, or keywords externally.

## Discovery Output

For each opportunity, include:

- link
- community/source
- recency when available
- why it fits
- rules/norms summary when available
- answer-shaped local draft or outline
- explicit manual-only note

## Poor Fit Signals

Skip discovery or mark low fit when:

- the post is mostly conceptual or announcement-only
- the opportunity is old, off-topic, locked, or promotional-sensitive
- community rules prohibit self-promotion or require participation history
- the source would require private/confidential details to answer well
